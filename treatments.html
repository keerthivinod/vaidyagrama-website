The Foundation

What is Panchakarma?

Panchakarma — literally "five actions" — is the cornerstone of classical Ayurvedic medicine. It is a systematic, physician-guided process of deep purification and rejuvenation that removes accumulated toxins (Ama) from the body's deepest tissues.

Unlike symptomatic treatments, Panchakarma addresses root causes. It resets the body's natural intelligence, restores doshic balance, and creates conditions for sustainable healing. The five classical procedures — Vamana (therapeutic emesis), Virechana (purgation), Basti (medicated enemas), Nasya (nasal administration), and Raktamokshana (blood letting) — are selected based on your individual constitution (Prakriti) and current imbalance (Vikriti).

At Vaidyagrama, every Panchakarma program begins with a thorough physician consultation and ends with a carefully designed post-treatment regime (Paschat Karma) to ensure the healing is stable and lasting.

Daily Life

A Typical Day at Vaidyagrama

The rhythm of the day is itself a medicine. Here is what a typical treatment day looks like — though your schedule will be personalised by your physician.

5:30

Wake Up & Morning Routine

Gentle rising in alignment with nature's rhythms. Personal morning routine (Dinacharya) as prescribed by your physician — oil pulling, tongue scraping, warm water.

6:00

Yoga & Morning Chanting

Group yoga session followed by Vedic chanting. Gentle, therapeutic movements appropriate for your condition and prescribed by your care team.

7:00

Physician Consultation

Daily or scheduled consultation with your senior physician — monitoring progress, adjusting treatment, discussing observations.

8:00

Ayurvedic Breakfast

Freshly prepared Ayurvedic food — warm, wholesome, aligned with your Prakriti and treatment requirements. No compromises on food quality or preparation methods.

9–12

Treatment Sessions

Your prescribed treatment procedures — Abhyanga (oil massage), Shirodhara, Basti, Virechana, or other Panchakarma treatments. Administered by trained therapists under physician guidance.

12:30

Ayurvedic Lunch

The principal meal of the day — prepared fresh, seasonal, and aligned with your treatment protocol. Eaten mindfully, in quiet.

2:00

Rest (Vishrama)

Afternoon rest is an integral part of the healing protocol — not optional. This period allows the body to integrate and process the morning's treatments.

4:00

Activities & Consultations

Campus walks, garden time, optional educational sessions on Ayurveda, follow-up consultations, and any additional afternoon treatments as prescribed.

7:00

Ayurvedic Dinner

Light, easily digestible dinner — appropriate for the evening digestive capacity. Served early to allow for proper rest.

8:00

Satsang (Evening Gathering)

Evening chanting, prayer, or group gathering — the spiritual dimension of healing that anchors the day and prepares the mind for deep rest.

Note: Schedule varies by program (Panchakarma vs WellnessNext) and treatment phase. Your physician will tailor the daily rhythm to your healing needs.
