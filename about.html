Our Story

Born from a Vision to Restore Authentic Ayurveda

Vaidyagrama was established in 2008 as a dream seen by a group of four young vaidyas and two management experts. During an era when global interest in ancient wisdom and Ayurveda was growing significantly, they envisioned a place where classical Ayurveda could be practised in its fullest, most authentic form -- without compromise. The name "Vaidyagrama" itself translates to "healing village," reflecting this core intention.

Situated in the green outskirts of Coimbatore, South India, surrounded by mountains and natural forest, the campus was designed as a self-contained healing ecosystem. Rather than creating a transactional healthcare facility, the founders chose a different path -- one centred on healing relationships, where environment, food, medicine, rhythm, and human connection all align in service of restoration.

Under the medical leadership of Dr. Ramkumar, Dr. Ramadas, Dr. Harikrishnan, Dr. Harikumar, and Dr. Ramanandan, and with the guidance of life coaches Dr. J M Sampath and Dr. Kalpana Sampath, Vaidyagrama grew from a shared vision into a community-centred healing village that today serves patients from around the world.

Our Philosophy

Authentic Ayurveda for Universal Wellbeing

Sarve Bhavantu Sukhinah -- May all beings be happy.

At Vaidyagrama, healing is not a transaction -- it is a relationship. We believe Ayurveda is not merely a medical system but a complete philosophy of life, one that honours the body as a sacred instrument, the mind as a field to be cultivated, and the spirit as the source of all healing. Every element of the village is designed with this intention: the architecture follows Vastu shastra principles, the construction maximises environmental sustainability, and the community-centred approach ensures that every patient is held in care by the entire village.

Your physician is also your guide, your therapist also your companion, and the land itself your medicine. Clinical precision and spiritual sensitivity are not opposites -- they are partners in the truest healing.

What Makes Us Different

The Healing Village

Eco-Sustainable Campus

Built with eco-friendly mud blocks following Kerala Nalukettu style architecture, the campus integrates maximum environmental sustainability. Rainwater harvesting, solar energy, organic farming, and natural construction methods ensure that healing begins with the environment itself.

Vastu-Designed Courtyards

The facility houses 48 rooms divided into four clusters, with 12 rooms per medical director. Each cluster features four patient units and two treatment rooms arranged around a central courtyard, designed according to Vastu shastra principles for optimal healing energy.

Ashram Atmosphere

Life at Vaidyagrama follows a rhythmic, purposeful schedule -- morning prayers at the "Brahmakamalam," Yoga nidra sessions, Satsangs, nature walks, Ayurveda cooking demonstrations, and monthly special rituals and homams. This rhythm itself is therapeutic.

NABH Accreditation

Vaidyagrama is an 80-room, 120-bedded NABH-accredited facility -- the highest quality standard for healthcare in India. This ensures clinical safety, protocol adherence, patient rights, and international-grade standards without compromising the authenticity of classical Ayurveda.

Quality & Safety

NABH Accreditation — What It Means for You

The National Accreditation Board for Hospitals & Healthcare Providers (NABH) is part of the Quality Council of India and sets the gold standard for healthcare quality across India. Receiving NABH accreditation is a rigorous process -- it means Vaidyagrama has been independently assessed and found to meet exacting standards across clinical care, patient safety, infection control, documentation, and ethics.

For patients, NABH accreditation means you can trust that you are receiving care in a facility that follows international-grade safety protocols -- without compromising the authenticity of classical Ayurveda.

  • ✓ Standardised clinical protocols
  • ✓ Patient rights and confidentiality
  • ✓ Infection control and hygiene standards
  • ✓ Qualified, credentialed medical staff
  • ✓ Grievance redressal mechanisms

Beyond Healing

Associated Initiatives

Vaidyagrama's commitment extends beyond the treatment room into the wider community, caring for children, elders, animals, and the environment.

Punarnava Ayurveda Trust

Conducts free medical and eye camps for the underserved. The "Annam" project provides daily meals to 100+ elderly and destitute people in the surrounding community, embodying the belief that nourishment is the first medicine.

Balagrama

A children's home housing 30 orphaned and disadvantaged village children, providing them with education, nutrition, and a loving family environment. Balagrama ensures these children grow up with dignity and opportunity.

Lakshmigrama

A sanctuary for 35+ native cows, supporting natural farming and preserving indigenous cattle breeds. The cows provide Panchagavya products used in traditional Ayurvedic preparations, completing the circle of sustainable healing.

Nivrittigrama

A community living concept promoting meaningful aging. Nivrittigrama envisions a space where elders can live with purpose, dignity, and connection -- supported by Ayurvedic care and surrounded by a vibrant, intergenerational community.
